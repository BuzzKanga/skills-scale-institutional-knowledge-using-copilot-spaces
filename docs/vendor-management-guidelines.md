# Vendor Management Guidelines

This document provides guidance on managing vendor and contractor relationships, deliverable tracking, and escalation procedures.

> **Related docs**: [Roles & Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) | [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md) | [Risk Management & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md)

## Overview

Effective vendor management ensures that external partnerships deliver value, meet timelines, and align with project goals. This guide outlines responsibilities, processes, and templates for managing vendor relationships at OctoAcme.

---

## Vendor / Contract Manager Responsibilities

The Vendor / Contract Manager is responsible for:

- **Relationship Management**: Serve as the primary point of contact for vendor communications
- **Contract Oversight**: Track contract terms, deliverables, and timelines
- **Deliverable Tracking**: Monitor vendor progress and quality of deliverables
- **Risk Management**: Identify and escalate vendor-related risks
- **Financial Coordination**: Work with Finance on invoicing and payments
- **Compliance**: Ensure vendor compliance with security and legal requirements

### Status Definitions

- **Completed**: Deliverable has been received and accepted
- **Blocked**: External dependency preventing progress

## Deliverable Tracking

### Deliverable Checklist

For each vendor deliverable:

- [ ] Deliverable scope clearly defined
- [ ] Acceptance criteria documented
- [ ] Due date confirmed
- [ ] Quality standards specified
- [ ] Review process defined
- [ ] Delivery format agreed

### Deliverable Acceptance Process

1. **Receive Deliverable**: Vendor submits deliverable
2. **Initial Review**: Vendor Manager performs initial quality check
3. **Technical Review**: Engineering/QA reviews technical aspects
4. **Acceptance Decision**:
   - ✅ Accepted: Deliverable meets all criteria
   - ⚠️ Conditional: Minor issues to address
   - ❌ Rejected: Major issues requiring rework
5. **Feedback**: Provide feedback to vendor
6. **Sign-off**: Document acceptance and update tracking

## Escalation Path

### Escalation Levels

| Level | Issue Type | Who to Contact | Response Time |
|-------|------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Level 1** | Day-to-day issues | Vendor Manager | 1-2 business days |
| **Level 2** | Quality or timeline concerns | Project Manager | 2-3 business days |
| **Level 3** | Contract disputes, major delays | Finance + Legal | 3-5 business days |
| **Level 4** | Critical business impact | Executive Sponsor | Immediate |

### Escalation Process

1. **Document the Issue**
   - Clear description of the problem
   - Impact on project/timeline
   - Actions already taken
   - Requested resolution

2. **Escalate to Vendor Manager**
   - Discuss with vendor first
   - Attempt resolution at vendor level
   - Document all communications

3. **Escalate to Project Manager**
   - If vendor resolution fails
   - Project Manager engages vendor management
   - Formal escalation letter if needed

4. **Escalate to Finance + Legal**
   - Contract-related disputes
   - Payment issues
   - SLA breaches requiring formal action

5. **Escalate to Executive Sponsor**
   - Critical business impact
   - Relationship-ending decisions
   - Major financial implications

## Communication Guidelines

- Use a shared project tracking tool when possible
